# Frontend Skill

Use this skill for CycleCare frontend work: landing experience, onboarding, forms, paywall, dashboard, notifications, privacy screens, and App Store review-safe UI.

## Product Positioning

CycleCare should feel like a calm premium wellness app, not a medical dashboard.

- Core promise: help a woman understand her cycle and choose what to share with her partner.
- Emotional tone: soft, intimate, clear, respectful, never infantilizing.
- User control is central: consent, privacy, contact choice, and message confirmation must stay visible.
- Avoid making the partner the main user. He receives guidance because she allows it.

## Visual Direction

Aim for pure, modern, high-trust, mobile-first design.

- Use generous spacing, restrained cards, glassy or soft surfaces, and strong typographic hierarchy.
- Prefer warm off-white, blush, plum, clay, rose, and subtle sage accents.
- Avoid childish pink overload, medical blue dashboards, aggressive gradients, and cluttered onboarding.
- Use rounded shapes where the experience should feel soft, but keep forms precise and readable.
- Use large first-screen storytelling, then compact functional screens.

## UX Principles

- The first screen should sell the feeling: harmony, understanding, privacy, and control.
- Onboarding should ask for only what is needed: first name, last period date, cycle length, period length, needs.
- Every sharing action needs explicit confirmation.
- The woman must always see what will be sent before it is sent.
- Never imply certainty about cycle phase; use “probablement”, “estimé”, or “indicatif”.
- Keep medical disclaimers short, visible, and calm.

## Paid Plan UX

CycleCare’s paid plan is Couple+.

- Free plan: limited number of cycle info sends per month.
- Couple+: extended sends so she can share guidance throughout the cycle.
- Partner reply is optional and secondary: one short reply per notification only if she enables it.
- In iOS copy, paid features must be described as unlocked through Apple in-app purchase / StoreKit.
- Do not mention external payment providers in app UI.
- The paywall should explain the benefit before the price: “share through the whole cycle”.

## App Store-Safe Requirements

Preserve these in UI and flows:

- Consent before sharing sensitive cycle information.
- Easy disable, delete, and export controls.
- No medical diagnosis claims.
- No advertising or marketing reuse of health data.
- No dark patterns around paid features or consent.
- Clear privacy copy before account creation or sharing.

## Interface Checklist

Before finishing any frontend change, check:

- Mobile layout has no overlapping text or controls.
- Primary action is obvious.
- Forms look calm and trustworthy.
- Privacy and consent are not hidden.
- Paywall benefits are clear without pressure.
- Text sounds premium and human in French.
- The app still works without data entered.
- The design feels like an installable app, not a local prototype.

## Copy Style

Use French copy that is concise, warm, and confident.

Good examples:

- “Transformer le cycle en langage d’amour.”
- “Partager les repères de tout le cycle.”
- “La personne qui suit son cycle garde le contrôle.”
- “Une réponse courte, seulement si elle l’autorise.”

Avoid:

- “Surveiller sa femme.”
- “Prédire avec précision.”
- “Comprendre son humeur.”
- “Accès illimité au cycle.”
